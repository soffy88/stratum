## 原文

The Sharpe ratio, despite its widespread adoption, suffers from several
well-documented limitations when applied to financial returns that exhibit
non-normality, autocorrelation, or finite-sample bias. Bailey, Borwein, and
Lopez de Prado (2014) introduced the Deflated Sharpe Ratio (DSR), which
corrects for the inflationary effect of multiple testing on in-sample performance.
